The people behind every crate.

Stackly began in a two-car garage in 2014 with one promise: sell only what we'd feed our own families. Today that promise runs through 340 farms, four fulfillment hubs, and 52,000 kitchens.

Our Origin

Started with a broken supply chain, fixed one farm at a time.

Co-founders Elena Marsh and Tobias Reyes met at a farmers' market, both frustrated by the same thing: produce that traveled further than most people's vacations, losing flavor and nutrients along the way. Stackly's first rule — nothing travels more than 150 miles from soil to doorstep — still holds today.

What started as a Saturday delivery run to 40 neighbors is now a daily operation spanning the Hudson Valley, with the same obsession over freshness that filled that first garage.

Common Questions

Everything you're probably wondering.

Can't find what you're looking for? Our Help Center team answers within the hour, every day of the week.

How do you enforce the 150-mile rule?+

Every farm's address is logged and distance-checked against each fulfillment hub before onboarding. If a farm falls outside the radius, we don't list it — no exceptions, no waivers.

Are all products organic?+

No — and we won't pretend otherwise. Roughly 70% of our produce is certified organic. Everything else is clearly labeled conventional so you can choose what's right for you.

What happens to the reusable crates?+

Leave your empty crate out on your next delivery day and our driver swaps it for a full one. Crates are washed and reused an average of 40 times before retirement.

Do you hire seasonally?+

Yes, particularly for harvest-season packing roles at our hubs. Openings are posted on our Careers page and shared first with past seasonal staff.
